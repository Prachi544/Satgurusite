Griha Pravesh Puja

A Vedic housewarming ceremony performed before or right after moving into a new home. It purifies the space, removes vaastu doshas, and invites prosperity and peace for everyone who will live there.

Why Griha Pravesh Matters

In Vedic tradition, a new home carries the energy of everyone who built it and everything that happened on the land before. Griha Pravesh is performed to cleanse that energy, seek the blessings of Vastu Purush (the deity of the dwelling), and formally welcome positivity, health, and abundance into the household.

It's traditionally done on an auspicious date and time (muhurat), which your pandit can help you choose based on the family's horoscope and the local panchang.

How the Ceremony Unfolds

Step 1

Ganpati & Kalash Sthapana

The ceremony opens with an invocation to Lord Ganesh and placement of the sacred kalash at the entrance.

Step 2

Vastu Shanti Havan

A sacred fire is lit and mantras are chanted to pacify Vastu Purush and cleanse the home's energy.

Step 3

Navgrah & Home Blessing

The nine planets are worshipped, followed by a walk through every room with holy water and a diya.

Step 4

Boiling of Milk

A symbolic ritual where milk is boiled over on the new stove to invite abundance into the kitchen.

Step 5

Aarti & Prasad

The ceremony closes with a final aarti, and prasad is distributed among family and guests.

Step 6

Family Blessing

Your pandit offers personal blessings to the family for health, harmony, and prosperity in the new home.
